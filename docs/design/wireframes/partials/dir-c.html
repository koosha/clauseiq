C · Strengths
  • Zero navigation — momentum preserved across dozens of queries.
  • Live preview lets users verify without leaving the flow.
  • Scratchpad dock is a persistent progress indicator.
  • Scales naturally to post-MVP features (compare, diff).
C · Tradeoffs
  • Information density can feel overwhelming on first load.
  • Small-screen users (13" laptop) lose the preview pane.
  • Contract is shown in a narrow column, not full-page — less "document reading" feel.
  • More surface area to engineer for MVP.

08

Recommendation & next steps

My pick: Direction C, with B's command bar grafted on

Why C. Lawyers run dozens of queries in one session. Page-based navigation (A) breaks momentum; a collapsed drawer (B) buries the scratchpad just when you want it loudest. The workbench keeps all three atoms — filters, results, preview — in view, and the scratchpad dock turns pinning into a progress bar.

Why graft in B. The palette's instant-focus entry and keyboard-first affordances are too good to lose. Keep the workbench as the "home" layout, open the command palette on ⌘K / / for power moves (jump to a specific family, reopen a prior query, paste-and-match).

When to keep A. If early user tests show in-house counsel over‑45 struggle with dense three-column layouts, A is the safe fallback. Everything we build in C downgrades cleanly to A — filters + results list + a dedicated reader page.

What's not in these wireframes (on purpose)

  • Auth, team admin, ingestion status. Explicitly deferred per the MVP plan.
  • OCR / scanned contract handling. Rejected upstream; no UI needed.
  • AI chat surface. Retrieval is the product. A chat window would invite scope creep.
  • Eval dashboard. Internal tool, lives outside the lawyer UI.
  • Word add-in. Phase 2. Today's finish line is a .docx download.

Open questions

Things I'd want your input on before hi-fi

  • Scratchpad persistence. One global scratchpad, or per "matter" (draft)? Lawyers likely want one per deal.
  • Citation style in export. Word comments, footnotes, or stripped out (so the draft is clean)? All three are viable — which do your users want?
  • Clause-family visibility. Is "limitation_of_liability" fine as-is, or does it need a pretty label ("Limitation of liability")? I'm leaning pretty.
  • Paste-and-match UX. Dedicated mode, or just let the palette accept pasted text and route automatically? Lean automatic.
  • Result count. The plan returns top-20. Is 20 the right number on screen, or should we show 10 and paginate?
